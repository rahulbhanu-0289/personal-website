"How I solved: Should I buy a petrol or diesel car?"

Rahul Bhanu, May 2020

Towards the end of 2015, my wife and I were planning to buy our first car. Following days of research, we came up with our top 3 prioritized choices. After the test drives, of course, we had lots of debates & discussions, but in the end, we finalized on Volkswagen’s Vento car model. Being 100% sure that I made the right choice, there was one thing which was not very clear to me: Should I buy a petrol or diesel variant of the car?

This question came up concerning the Indian market context, where diesel car variants are costlier than petrol variants however diesel prices are cheaper than petrol prices. As usual, I opened Google and started doing my research. Although I found some information online, it was not sufficient to answer the question I had. As an additional source to my research, I asked some of my family members, friends, and colleagues about it. I got a lot of different guesses & opinions, none of which answered my question, “should I buy a petrol or diesel variant of the car”. I concluded that deciding between car variants seemed to be a valid problem faced by many other people as well. This triggered the problem-solving part of my brain and I took it up as a challenge to find the solution.

I started thinking in detail about the problem I am trying to solve and brainstormed on possible alternative solutions and how I could design the right solution. In the process, I recognized that this decision of choosing between the car variants involves broadly two categories of factors. Market factors (petrol & diesel price difference, the difference in prices of car models etc.) & User factors (monthly car mileage, number of years user wishes to use the same car etc.). Considering heterogeneous answers to these factors and looking at the target user group, I decided to build a calculator that would simplify the user’s decision-making process. My obvious choice to build this calculator was excel as it is not only easily accessible but also users can edit it depending on their special factors. After a few iterations & feedbacks from test users, I finalized the first version of my product.

This simple product was successful among my peers and personal network. Developing this product, I got the satisfaction of creating something that everyone around me could benefit from.

(PS: This tool got me my dream car, Volkswagen’s Vento Petrol.)
